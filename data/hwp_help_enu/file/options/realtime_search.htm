Real Time Search
 

File > Options > Others

Real Time Search helps you quickly search a word at the same time you enter it, and insert AutoText which has already been added.

You can specify a dictionary to searh for the word and the type of AutoText.

Steps

To search dictionary in real time

  1. Click File > Options.
  2. Go to the Others tab in the Options dialog box.
  3. Click Real Time Search under the Search group.
  4. In the Real-Time Search dialog box, select E-K Essence from List for Search, and then click Set.
  5. Click Set in the Options dialog box.
  6. Enter 'office' in the document, then list of the search results appears under the entered word.

    Or, if you select the word as a block, list of the search results also appears with definitions.

  7. To see definitions for any word from the list, click one of the listed words.
  8. If you double-click a word or press the key for the number at the beginning of each word on your keyboard, the word is inserted in your document.

To insert AutoText in real time

  1. Enter 'Thinkfree Office NEO' and select as a block in the document.
  2. Click Quick Parts > AutoText > Add AutoText in the Insert tab.
  3. In the Add AutoText dialog box, the block will be registered in Original Word and the first letter in the block in Abbreviation.

    In this example, H will be displayed in Abbreviation and Thinkfree Office NEO in Original Word.

  4. Select Preserve Text Properties and click Add.
  5. Enter 'H' or select 'H' as a block in the document. Then, list of AutoText which has already been added appears.
  6. If you double-click AutoText or press the key for the number at the beginning of each AutoText on your keyboard, the AutoText is inserted in your document.

Note

Inserting the searched word or AutoText with the numeric key

The numbers at the beginning of each searched word indicate the numeric key, not the sequence of the search results. Therefore, you can insert one of the searched words in your document by pressing the key for the number at the beginning of each word on your keyboard.
