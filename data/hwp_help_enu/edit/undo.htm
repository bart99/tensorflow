Undo <CTRL+Z>
 

Home > Undo

You can revert back from completed actions in the process of document editing by canceling the previous command, deleting typed content, or retrieving deleted content.

User actions are remembered step by step. Therefore, you can revert back the edited document to its previous states by repeatedly running Undo.

Undo is a useful function that minimizes mistakes that the user makes while editing documents.

Steps

  1. Type text in the document on the edit screen or edit the document, for example, by changing fonts.
  2. Click Home > Undo.
  3. Check on the edit screen that the last action has been cancelled.
  4. Click Home > Redo.
  5. Check that the action cancelled by Undo has been redone.

Note

Undo and Redo

Click Home > Redo to redo the command or action cancelled by Undo.

Run Undo Continuously

The actions to Undo are a series of typing or editing actions that were performed by the user in the active document and remembered step by step. Running Undo repeatedly brings the same result as canceling the previous actions backward.

Nothing to Undo

When you create a new document or open a document by clicking Open, there is no editing in the document and thus there is no change when you click Home > Undo.

Undo is displayed blurred on menus and toolbars and cannot be used.

Empty Actions to Undo

When you are editing a document, up to 256 actions to undo are remembered step by step. Once you complete document editing, you can click Close to delete all the actions remembered for Undo.

Range of Undo

Actions remembered for Undo include content typed and edited in the active document.

When you edit multiple documents at the same time, actions to Undo are remembered separately for each document.

Also, actions that are not related to editing the active document, which include Save, Customize, and Zoom, are not remembered for Undo.
