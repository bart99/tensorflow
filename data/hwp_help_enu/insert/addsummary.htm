Phonetic Guide
 

Home > Phonetic Guide

A phonetic guide is text inserted below or above a word in the body text to show the source of quoted material or to provide short supplementary comments about content mentioned in the body text.

Phonetic Guide in Hwp is also useful for displaying side dots, Japanese particles after nouns and Chinese phonetic symbols.

Steps

  1. Type Hwp on the edit screen and select it as a block.
  2. Click Home > Phonetic Guide.
  3. As the Phonetic Guide dialog box opens, type Hwp in Original Word. Type Korean Word Processor in the Phonetic Guide dialog box.
  4. Select Top in Phonetic Guide Position and click Insert.
  5. On the edit screen, a phonetic guide that displays Korean Word Processor will be inserted above the words Hwp.

Note

Properties of Phonetic Guide

Phonetic guides follow the properties of the original words and are aligned from the center of the original words. The text size of phonetic guides is fixed as 1/2 of the size of their original words and thus depends on the size of the original words. A phonetic guide is as big as the original word.

Difference Between Footnote/Endnote and Phonetic Guide

Footnotes are inserted at the bottom of the page that has footnote numbers. Endnotes are inserted at the end of a chapter or book, instead of the pages that have endnote numbers. Phonetic Guide is located right below or above their original words.
