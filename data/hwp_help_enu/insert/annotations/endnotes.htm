Insert Endnote <CTRL+ALT+D>
 

References > Footnotes > Insert Endnote

You can create endnotes for footnotes designed for providing short supplementary comments about content in the body text or showing the source of quoted material.

The endnote will be at the end of the current section or document if you insert it. You can specify type of endnote numbers or location of endnotes in References > Footnotes > Footnote/Endnote Styles.

Steps

  1. In the body text, place the pointer behind a word for which you an endnote has to be inserted and click References > Footnotes > Insert Endnote.
  2. An endnote number will be inserted at the pointer and the pointer will move to the end of the document so that you can type content for the endnote. The Notes tab will be displayed.
  3. Type content next to the endnote number like 1) that is automatically inserted.
  4. Press SHIFT+ESC to return to the body text.

Note

Type Endnote Content

An endnote number will be inserted in a position of the body text where the pointer was and the pointer will move to a position where you will type endnote content by using Insert Endnote.

Endnote content is separate from the body text. You can specify in detail how to place endnote content in a page in References > Footnotes > Footnote/Endnote Styles.

Notes Toolbar

In the Notes tab that is displayed when you type endnote content, you can change frequently used styles in Footnote/Endnote Styles or point to another footnote and edit it.

Move Pointer Between Endnote and Body Text

To return to the body text after typing endnote content, press SHIFT+ESC or click Close in the Close group of the Notes tab. The pointer will move to the endnote number in the body text.

You can also point to the body text by clicking the body text.

Modify Endnote Styles

You can modify the endnote of the number by resting the pointer on an endnote number in the body text and double-clicking it when the pointer is changed to an endnote marker.

You can also modify the endnote by directly clicking it.

Or, click Previous Note and Next Note on the Notes toolbar.

Delete Notes

If you delete an endnote number in the body text, the endnote of that number will be deleted too.

You can delete endnote numbers by pressing DELETE or BACKSPACE, just like you delete regular texts.

Note Number

The endnote will be automatically numbered according to its position in the body text by inserting it in the body text.

You can change the type and style of endnote numbers in References > Footnotes > Footnote/Endnote Styles.

Caution

Restriction

You can insert endnotes only in the body text that includes a table or text box. You cannot insert endnotes in editing areas such as Header/Footer.

Advanced Usage

Convention

The text size of footnotes is usually 2 points smaller than that of the body text but should not be smaller than 8 points.

The text size of endnotes should be smaller than that of the body text but bigger than that of footnotes.

Footnote numbers which are added to the body text are Arabic numerals and must be in the form of superscript. The numbers in the footnote or endnote area are usually in the same size as footnotes or endnotes and do not use parentheses.
