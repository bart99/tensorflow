Insert Footnote <CTRL+ALT+F>
 

References > Footnotes > Insert Footnote

You can create footnotes for footnotes designed for providing short supplementary comments about content in the body text or showing the source of quoted material.

Footnotes are inserted at the bottom of the pages that have footnote numbers. You can specify type of footnote numbers or location of footnotes in References > Footnotes > Footnote/Endnote Styles.

Steps

  1. In the body text, place the pointer behind a word for which the footnote has to be inserted and click References > Footnotes > Insert Footnote.
  2. A footnote number will be inserted at the pointer and the pointer will move to the bottom of the page so that you can type content for the footnote. The Notes tab will display automatically.
  3. Type content next to the footnote number like 1) that is automatically inserted.
  4. Press SHIFT+ESC to return to the body text.

Note

Type Footnote Content

A footnote number will be inserted in a position of the body text where the pointer was by using Insert Footnote, and the pointer will move to a position where you will type the footnote content.

Footnote content is separate from the body text. You can specify in detail how to place footnote content in a page in References > Footnotes > Footnote/Endnote Styles.

Notes Toolbar

In the Notes tab that is displayed when you type footnote content, you can change frequently used styles in Footnote/Endnote Styles or point to another footnote and edit it.

Move Pointer Between Footnote and Body Text

To return to the body text after typing footnote content, press SHIFT+ESC or click Close in the Close group of the Notes tab. Then the pointer will move to the footnote number in the body text.

You can also point to the body text by clicking it.

Modify Footnote Styles

You can modify the footnote of the number by resting the pointer on a footnote number in the body text and double-clicking it when the pointer is changed to a footnote marker.

You can also modify the footnote by directly clicking it.

Or, click Previous Note and Next Note on the Notes toolbar.

Delete Notes

If you delete a footnote number in the body text, the footnote of that number will be deleted, too.

You can delete footnote numbers by pressing DELETE or BACKSPACE, just like when you delete regular texts.

Note Number

The footnote will be automatically numbered according to its position in the body text if you insert it in the body text.

You can change the type or style of footnote numbers in References > Footnotes > Footnote/Endnote Styles.

Style of Footnote Content

The style will automatically apply to the footnote as soon as you type content if you insert a footnote and the active document has a style named English Name-Footnote on the style list.

So the footnote content you type will have a consistent style.

Footnote Inside Table or Text Box

You can insert a footnote inside a table or text box. The content of the footnote inserted in a table or text box will be inserted at the bottom of the page where the table or text box is.

Caution

Restriction

You can insert footnotes only in the body text that includes a table or text box. You cannot insert footnotes in editing areas such as Header/Footer.

Advanced Usage

Convention

The text size of footnotes is usually 2 points smaller than that of the body text but should not be smaller than 8 points.

The text size of endnotes should be smaller than that of the body text but bigger than that of footnotes.

Footnote numbers which are added to the body text are Arabic numerals and must be in the form of superscript. The numbers in the footnote or endnote area are usually in the same size as footnotes or endnotes and do not use parentheses.
