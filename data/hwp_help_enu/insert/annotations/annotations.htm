Footnotes
 

References > Footnotes

You can use footnotes and endnotes to show the source of quoted material or to provide short supplementary comments about content mentioned in the body text.

The footnotes function is important for inserting footnotes or endnotes in a document for various purposes.

A Hidden Note is a footnote which is designed for editors of the file but not displayed on the screen or print-out. Although not part of the body text, it is added to the file to provide comments for editors.

Note

Difference Between Footnote and Endnote

The difference between Footnote and Endnote is their position.

Footnotes are located at the bottom of the pages where footnote numbers are inserted. Endnotes are inserted at the end of a chapter or book, instead of the pages that have endnote numbers.

The strength of footnotes is that it is easy to refer to them because they are on the same page as the body text. The strength of endnotes is that you can see all of them in one place.

Automatic Numbering

You do not need to worry about the order of footnote or endnote numbers because Hwp automatically numbers them in the body text and footnotes or endnotes.

Footnote Number and Endnote Number

Footnotes and endnotes are numbered separately because footnotes and endnotes are treated as two separate notes in a file.

Restart Numbering

To number footnotes/endnotes with new numbers from a certain position, click Page Layout > Restart Numbering.
